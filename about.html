Our Story

About the Property

A century of American manufacturing heritage, reimagined for Springfield's next chapter.

The History

A Legacy of Precision Manufacturing

The property at 100 River Street was originally built and operated by the Fellows Gear Shaper Company, a manufacturer of precision gear-cutting machinery that was a cornerstone employer in Springfield for over a century.

The campus includes the main production facility (~255,000 SF on two stories with a three-story office wing), a cafeteria building (three stories with attached garage, connected by pedestrian catwalk), a boiler house, and the historic 1898 House.

The Vision

Transforming Springfield's Largest Asset

100 River Street Venture, LLC is transforming this brownfield into a vibrant mixed-use hub:

  • Full-service restaurant and event space with riverfront dining
  • Brewery with indoor/outdoor seating and Black River views
  • Manufacturing and production spaces
  • Self-storage facility
  • Retail storefronts and cosmetology services
  • Professional office space
  • Green space and community gathering areas

Springfield Medical Center and an on-site pharmacy already call 100 River Street home.

The Campus

Five Distinct Buildings, One Campus

Main Production Facility

~255,000 SF, two stories with a three-story office wing. High ceilings, heavy structural capacity, and a flexible open floor plan.

Cafeteria Building

Three-story former workforce cafeteria with attached garage. Connected to the main building by pedestrian catwalk. Planned for brewery operations with riverfront dining.

Boiler House

Standalone outbuilding suitable for auxiliary commercial or mechanical use.

1898 House

Historic structure on campus grounds with potential for office or retail conversion.
